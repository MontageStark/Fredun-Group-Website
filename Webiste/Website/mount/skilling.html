Transforming Lives With Skills

At Fredun Group, we understand that employment begins with ability. That’s why our skilling programs — especially under Inkling India — are tailored to help the differently-abled, unemployed youth, and single mothers acquire practical, job-ready skills. We partner with vocational institutes and employers to align our training with real-world opportunities. Each program includes soft skills development, interview preparation, and basic financial literacy. Flexible learning formats ensure that participants can balance personal responsibilities while upskilling. Post-training job placements and internship support help bridge the gap between learning and earning. Our mission is to create pathways to dignity, independence, and lasting change through meaningful employment.

Inkling India – Skills for All

Inkling India provides personalized skilling modules for people with disabilities and those lacking family support. From spoken English and digital literacy to tailoring, painting, and basic tech repairs — every course is hands-on and designed to build income potential. All training is accessible, inclusive, and free. We ensure that training environments are mobility-friendly and equipped with assistive tools like sign language support and screen readers. Individual progress is monitored closely to adapt learning methods and boost confidence. Guest sessions with successful alumni inspire and motivate current learners. Small batch sizes allow for focused attention and peer-to-peer collaboration. At Inkling India, we believe that skills not only open doors to jobs — they unlock self-worth and hope.

Skilling Women for Financial Freedom

We actively conduct vocational training workshops for women in rural and urban slum areas. Programs include sewing, beauty & wellness, handicraft creation, and retail training. These skills help women start their own businesses or earn consistent income, creating independence and dignity. We also provide micro-finance guidance and support to help women launch and sustain their ventures. Training sessions are scheduled flexibly to accommodate household responsibilities. Successful participants often return as trainers, creating a cycle of empowerment. Community showcases and exhibitions give women a platform to sell their products and gain confidence. Through these initiatives, we aim to break economic barriers and uplift entire families, one skilled woman at a time.

Upskilling Youth for the Future

Our youth training programs include basic coding, mobile repairing, computer literacy, resume writing, and soft skills. These ensure that young individuals, especially those from underprivileged backgrounds, are ready for both employment and entrepreneurship opportunities. We collaborate with industry experts to keep our curriculum aligned with evolving market needs. Career guidance sessions help youth identify their strengths and choose suitable paths. Internships and job placement support bridge the gap between training and real-world experience. Special focus is placed on digital inclusion to prepare them for a tech-driven future. By investing in youth potential today, we’re building the skilled workforce of tomorrow.
